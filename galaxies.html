Galaxies

What are Galaxies?

Galaxies are massive systems of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter, bound together by gravity. They can vary widely in size and structure.

Types of Galaxies

1. Spiral Galaxies

Spiral galaxies feature a flat, rotating disk containing stars, gas, and dust, along with a central concentration of stars known as the bulge.

2. Elliptical Galaxies

Elliptical galaxies are shaped like ellipses and contain older stars with little interstellar gas and dust.

3. Irregular Galaxies

Irregular galaxies do not have a distinct regular shape, often resulting from gravitational interactions with other galaxies.

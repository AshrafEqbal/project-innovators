About the James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is the most powerful space telescope ever built. Designed to study the universe in infrared wavelengths, it allows astronomers to look further back in time and gather unprecedented data about celestial objects.

Where is it? JWST orbits around the Sun at a point known as L2, approximately 1.5 million kilometers from Earth.

Contributions: The telescope has revolutionized our understanding of the universe, providing insights into the formation of stars, galaxies, and planetary systems. It has already made remarkable discoveries, including the detection of water vapor in the atmospheres of exoplanets.

Partnership: JWST is a collaborative effort between NASA, the European Space Agency (ESA), and the Canadian Space Agency (CSA).

Explore Celestial Objects

Discover the wonders of the universe through the following categories:

  • Exoplanets: Planets beyond our solar system, some of which may harbor life.
  • Galaxies: Vast systems of stars, gas, and dust, each containing billions of stars.
  • Nebulas: Stunning clouds of gas and dust in space, often serving as stellar nurseries.

Latest Discoveries

Check out some of the latest discoveries made by the JWST:

  • First Images of Exoplanets: JWST has captured its first images of exoplanets, providing new insights into their atmospheres.
  • Galactic Evolution: The telescope has helped us understand how galaxies evolve over billions of years.
  • Dark Matter Studies: JWST is contributing to our understanding of dark matter and its role in galaxy formation.
